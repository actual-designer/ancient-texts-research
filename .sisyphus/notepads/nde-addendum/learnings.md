

## Section 10 Writing -- Key Patterns and Conventions

### Skeptical Analysis Framing
- Must present ALL major materialist explanations fairly and systematically.
- Each theory gets "The Claim," "What It Explains," and "The Gaps" subsections.
- The Archive's position: skeptical explanations are incomplete, not wrong.
- Never dismiss skeptical views; treat them as seriously as pro-NDE evidence.

### Required Citations
- DMT: Strassman (2001); Timmermann et al. (2018) -- controlled study showing NDE Scale overlap.
- Hypoxia: van Lommel -- no correlation with arrest duration.
- Terminal brain surges: Borjigin et al. (2013) -- rat gamma surge; Zemmar et al. (2022) -- single human case.
- Temporal lobe: Persinger (1983; 2001) -- TMS/God helmet.

### Summary Table Design
- Master table with columns: Theory | Explains | Fails to Explain | Key Source.
- Each row must be specific about what the theory covers and where it falls short.
- Veridical perception is the persistent gap across ALL materialist explanations.

### Conclusion Structure
- State formally: no single skeptical theory explains all features.
- Identify the three core tensions: structural consistency, veridical perception, lasting after-effects.
- End with transition to next section (extracerebral perception alternative).

### Tone and Vocabulary
- Clinical/forensic throughout. No spiritual or religious framing.
- "Materialist framework has not been falsified. It has been shown to be incomplete."
- Use "incomplete" rather than "wrong" or "refuted."

### Anti-AI-Slop Compliance
- No em dashes or en dashes used. Commas, periods, and line breaks only.
- No AI-sounding phrases ("delve", "it's important to note", etc.).
- Plain words: "use" not "utilize," "start" not "commence."
- Contractions used naturally.
- Varied sentence length.
- No filler openings.
