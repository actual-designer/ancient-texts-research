## 2. Correlation Methodology

This section establishes the evidentiary rules governing all NHI-to-Archive correlations. Without strict criteria, the framework devolves into unfalsifiable pattern-matching. Every correlation in this document is evaluated against the following four standards.

### Valid Correlation Criteria

A valid correlation requires specific structural overlap between a modern NHI claim and an ancient operational pattern described in the Archive. Thematic or symbolic resemblance is explicitly excluded. For instance, a report of non-consensual biological sampling conducted by rank-structured entities maps structurally to the census-and-extraction protocols recorded in the Atrahasis narrative. In contrast, a general claim of "beings from the sky" lacks the operational specificity required for a valid correlation.

### Evidence Quality Tiers

All source material is ranked by reliability and temporal proximity to the claimed event:

(a) **Ancient Texts**: Curated primary sources with established provenance. These narratives are fixed but filtered through pre-technological witness vocabulary, requiring reverse-engineering translation.

(b) **Modern NHI Testimony**: Oral reports, whistleblower accounts, and formal government testimony. These sources offer contemporary operational detail but suffer from a lack of independent verification and high susceptibility to fabrication, contamination, or psychological distortion.

(c) **Secondary Analysis**: Academic frameworks, journalistic summaries, and interpretive models. These provide useful structural vocabulary but are derivative; they cannot substitute for primary source or firsthand testimony.

### Confirmation Bias Guardrails

The central methodological risk is the "everything maps" fallacy. A claimed correlation is rejected if it depends on cherry-picked phrases stripped of context, requires heavy post-hoc reinterpretation to force a fit, or can be more simply explained by known psychological phenomena, folklore evolution, or cultural diffusion. Every proposed parallel must survive a minimal counter-explanation test before acceptance.

### Disconfirmation

The Archive framework is intentionally falsifiable. A disconfirming NHI pattern would include any of the following: a demonstrated absence of hierarchical command structures; technology that is biologically integrated rather than based on discrete, reusable tools; or explicit, verified communication of a strict non-interventionist policy regarding human genetic and social development. Consistent, reproducible NHI behavior that violates these predicted operational signatures would constitute grounds for rejecting the correlation hypothesis.
