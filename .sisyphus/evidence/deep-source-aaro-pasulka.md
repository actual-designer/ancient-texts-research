## AARO — All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office

### Source: Historical Record Report Volume I | AARO | March 2024

Context: This report represents AARO's congressionally mandated review of all official U.S. Government investigatory efforts since 1945. The following passages capture the office's official posture on extraterrestrial technology and reverse-engineering claims.

> "AARO found no evidence that any USG investigation, academic-sponsored research, or official review panel has confirmed that any sighting of a UAP represented extraterrestrial technology. All investigative efforts, at all levels of classification, concluded that most sightings were ordinary objects and phenomena and the result of misidentification."
> — Section II: Executive Summary, AARO Historical Record Report Vol. I, March 2024.

> "AARO found no empirical evidence for claims that the USG and private companies have been reverse-engineering extraterrestrial technology. AARO determined, based on all information provided to date, that claims involving specific people, known locations, technological tests, and documents allegedly involved in or related to the reverse-engineering of extraterrestrial technology, are inaccurate."
> — Section II: Executive Summary, AARO Historical Record Report Vol. I, March 2024.

> "AARO assesses that UAP sightings and reports of these sightings to USG organizations and claims that some UAP constitute extraterrestrial craft and that the USG has secured and is experimenting on extraterrestrial technology have been influenced by a range of cultural, political, and technological factors."
> — Section II: Executive Summary, AARO Historical Record Report Vol. I, March 2024.

### Source: FY24 Consolidated Annual Report on UAP | AARO / ODNI | November 2024

Context: AARO's annual report to Congress covering the reporting period May 1, 2023 to June 1, 2024. This passage restates the office's current official posture.

> "It is important to underscore that, to date, AARO has discovered no evidence of extraterrestrial beings, activity, or technology."
> — AARO FY24 Consolidated Annual Report on UAP, November 2024.

> "Of all the reports that AARO investigated and analyzed, none represent extraterrestrial or off-world technology. A small percentage of cases have potentially anomalous characteristics or concerning characteristics. AARO has kept Congress fully and currently informed of its findings. AARO's research continues on these cases."
> — AARO FY24 Consolidated Annual Report on UAP, November 2024.

## Pasulka — *American Cosmic: UFOs, Religion, Technology* (2019)

### Source: American Cosmic | D.W. Pasulka | Oxford University Press, 2019

Context: Pasulka, a professor of religious studies at UNC Wilmington, conducted a six-year ethnographic study of scientists, Silicon Valley entrepreneurs, and academics who believe in extraterrestrial intelligence. Her work frames the modern UFO phenomenon not as a fringe conspiracy but as an emergent religious system centered on technology.

#### (a) Disclosure as Cultural/Philosophical Phenomenon

> "The modern UFO narrative has become a widespread worldview thanks to an infrastructure of technology including online communities and numerous media outlets promulgating various versions of this narrative to broad audiences virally. As a result, traditional religious notions have become blended with UFO-based interpretations."
> — Pasulka, *American Cosmic*, p. 15 (cited in review, Project MUSE).

> "American Cosmic explores the intriguing question of how people interpret unexplainable experiences, and argues that the media is replacing religion as a cultural authority that offers believers answers about non-human intelligent life."
> — Publisher description, Oxford University Press, 2019.

#### (b) Technology and NHI Convergence

> "Technology informs a widespread and growing religiosity focused on UFOs."
> — Pasulka, *American Cosmic*, p. 3.

> "The UFO is considered by believers to be advanced technology. As a result, technology itself is a sacred medium, as well as the sacred object, of this new religiosity."
> — Pasulka, *American Cosmic*, Introduction (cited in LARB review, July 2019).

> "Like the Spiritualists of the nineteenth century, believers see technology as a portal or a frequency shift that allows humans to connect to other minds, human or extraterrestrial, as well as to places outside of the current understanding of space-time. Therefore, not only is the technological infrastructure the basis for widespread belief in UFOs, through media technologies and other mechanisms, but also technology itself is a sacred medium, as well as the sacred object, of this new religiosity."
> — Pasulka, *American Cosmic*, Introduction (cited in preview extract).

#### (c) Academic Framing of the Phenomenon

> "This book is about contemporary religion, using as a case study the phenomenon known as the UFO. It is also about technology. These may seem like completely unrelated topics, but they are intimately connected. They are connected because social and economic infrastructures shape the ways in which people practice religions."
> — Pasulka, *American Cosmic*, Introduction.

> "When I began to focus on modern reports of UFO sightings and events, I was immediately immersed in a world where the religious impulse was alive and the formation of a new, unique form of religion was in process. I was observing it as it happened. Carl Jung put it well. Referring to the modern phenomenon of flying saucers, he wrote, 'We have here a golden opportunity of seeing how a legend is formed.'"
> — Pasulka, *American Cosmic*, Introduction.

> "The participants are anonymous because of the stigma that is often associated with UFOs and belief in them, but also because there were classified government programs in which the phenomenon was studied, necessitating secrecy among the participants."
> — Pasulka, *American Cosmic*, Introduction.

---

**Analyst Note:** These two sources operate at opposite poles of the same discourse. AARO represents the institutional denial posture, maintaining that no UAP case has been confirmed as extraterrestrial and that cultural factors drive belief. Pasulka, working from religious studies rather than intelligence analysis, treats the very same cultural factors not as debunking evidence but as the object of study, demonstrating that belief in NHI has become functionally indistinguishable from religious systems, complete with sacred objects, priestly classes (the "Invisible College"), and eschatological narratives. The Archive holds both positions as data points, noting that AARO's conclusions are a single institutional reading among many.
