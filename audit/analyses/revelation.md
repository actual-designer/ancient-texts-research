Those not found in the "book of life" (a pre-approved registry) are terminated in the "lake of fire" (the second death -- permanent dissolution rather than ongoing torment).

### Plausible Mechanism

**Projected rehabilitation protocol with containment, testing, and final disposition phases.** The "1,000 years" is a period of contained stability. The release is a deliberate system test. The final judgment is a comprehensive disposition assessment: approved entities continue in the new system; unapproved entities are terminated permanently. The "second death" is final destruction, not eternal torture -- permanent removal from the system.

## INCIDENT 12: New Jerusalem -- Replacement Habitat Specification

### Source Material

> "And I saw a new heaven and a new earth: for the first heaven and the first earth were passed away; and there was no more sea." (21:1)

> "And I John saw the holy city, new Jerusalem, coming down from God out of heaven, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband." (21:2)

> "And he that talked with me had a golden reed to measure the city, and the gates thereof, and the wall thereof. And the city lieth foursquare, and the length is as large as the breadth: and he measured the city with the reed, twelve thousand furlongs. The length and the breadth and the height of it are equal." (21:15-16)

> "And the building of the wall of it was of jasper: and the city was pure gold, like unto clear glass." (21:18)

> "And the city had no need of the sun, neither of the moon, to shine in it: for the glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb is the light thereof." (21:23)

### Mainstream Interpretation

The eternal dwelling place of the redeemed -- a symbolic, perfect city representing God's presence with His people.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

A **precision-engineered orbital or extraterrestrial habitat** deployed after the planetary sterilization is complete (see [ezekiel-merkabah.md](ezekiel-merkabah.md): temple blueprints). Specifications:

| Dimension | Measurement | Conversion |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Shape | Cube | 12,000 furlongs per side |
| Side length | 12,000 furlongs | ~1,500 miles / ~2,400 km |
| Wall height | 144 cubits | ~216 feet / ~66 meters |
| Wall material | Jasper | Crystalline composite |
| Surface | Pure gold, transparent | Metallic alloy with optical clarity |
| Foundation | 12 layered precious stones | Multi-spectral structural layers |
| Gates | 12 pearls, 3 per side | Access points with luminescent entry structures |
| Internal illumination | Self-generated | Internal power source, no external star required |
| Water source | River from central throne | Internal water recycling/synthesis system |
| Medical resource | Tree of Life with monthly fruit | Regenerative biological production system |

The city is a **tetrahedral or cubic megastructure** with a side length of ~1,500 miles -- comparable in scale to a small planet or large orbital habitat. The "no temple therein" specification (21:22) indicates the entire structure is the command presence -- no separate worship space because the command authority is distributed throughout. The 12 gates of single pearl suggest single-piece crystalline entry structures. The layered precious-stone foundations suggest a multi-spectral or multi-functional structural base.

The "tree of life" on either side of the river (22:2) with "twelve manner of fruits, and yielded her fruit every month" is a continuous biomedical production system. Its leaves are "for the healing of the nations" -- a healthcare infrastructure integrated into the habitat.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Pre-fabricated orbital or extraterrestrial habitat, post-sterilization replacement for the current planetary biosphere.** The cubic dimensions (1,500-mile cube) describe a massive space habitat or terraformed structure. The self-illumination, internal water cycle, integrated medical production (Tree of Life), and access control (gates with angelic operators) describe a self-contained, permanently operated environment designed to replace the previous biological habitat.
