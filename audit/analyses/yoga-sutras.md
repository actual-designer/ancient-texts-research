## Executive Summary

The Yoga Sūtras of Patañjali (estimated ~400 BCE, based on linguistic and philosophical analysis) are the most systematic surviving **operator training manual** in the Archive. Unlike the narrative texts that describe events, entities, and technologies, the Sūtras are a **procedural document** — a step-by-step protocol for human operators to achieve modified cognitive states, develop supernormal capabilities, and ultimately decouple from baseline psycho-biological constraints.

Under the Amnesia hypothesis, the text documents:

1. A **consciousness lockdown protocol** (*citta-vṛtti-nirodha*, I.2) — systematic suppression of baseline cognitive operations
2. A **reference entity** (*Īśvara*, I.24-28) — a special being outside normal system constraints, used as a training anchor
3. A **cognitive lock-in diagnostic** (*kleśas*, II.3-9) — five afflictions that bind operators to the standard cognitive baseline
4. An **eight-stage operator training curriculum** (*aṣṭāṅga yoga*, II.29) — ethical, physiological, and cognitive conditioning sequence
5. A **supernormal capability catalog** (*vibhūti pāda*, III.1-56) — 40+ specific operator powers achievable through consciousness restructuring
6. A **system decoupling protocol** (*kaivalya*, IV.34) — permanent liberation from baseline cognitive constraints

The Sūtras are unique in the Archive as a **first-person operable manual**, not a third-person narrative. The operator reads and executes the protocol directly — no non-human intermediary required. This is the closest surviving document to a **usable piece of precursor technology**.

## INCIDENT 01: The Definition — Consciousness Lockdown Protocol

### Source Material

> **yogaḥ cittavṛtti nirodhaḥ** (I.2)
> "The skill of yoga is demonstrated by the conscious non-operation of the vibrational modes of the mento-emotional energy."

> **tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe avasthānam** (I.3)
> "Then the perceiver is situated in his own form."

> **vṛtti sārūpyam itaratra** (I.4)
> "At other times, there is conformity with the mento-emotional energy."

### Mainstream Interpretation

Yoga is defined as the stilling of mental fluctuations, leading to realization of the true self.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

Sūtra I.2 is a **functional definition** of a specific operational state: the operator deliberately suppresses all vibrational activity of the *citta* (mento-emotional energy field). Sūtras I.3-I.4 describe the before/after:

- **After suppression** (I.3): The perceiver exists in its own unmediated form — raw consciousness without content, direct access to baseline reality
- **Before suppression** (I.4): The perceiver is constantly merged with its own cognitive operations — the signal is lost in the noise

This is not a mystical claim but a **measurable change in cognitive operating mode**. The text describes a state transition from "merged with processing" to "pure perception" — a decoupling of the observer function from its own computational substrate. The five types of *vṛttis* (vibrations) catalogued in I.6 — correct perception, incorrect perception, imagination, sleep, and memory — represent **the full range of baseline cognitive operations**. Each is a processing mode that can be selectively suppressed.

Three methods for achieving suppression are given (I.7-11): direct perception (*pratyakṣa*), inference (*anumāna*), and reliable testimony (*āgama*) — this is a **calibration protocol** where the operator confirms correct system understanding before attempting the suppression procedure.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Consciousness-mode switching protocol.** The operator learns to suppress default cognitive processing to expose unmediated awareness. The five *vṛttis* map to standard cognitive operations that can be disabled through systematic training. The result (I.3) is a state of direct perception uncolored by interpretation — a cognitive transparency mode.

## INCIDENT 02: Īśvara — The Reference Entity

### Source Material

> **kleśa karma vipāka āśayaiḥ aparāmṛṣṭaḥ puruṣaviśeṣaḥ Īśvaraḥ** (I.24)
> "The Supreme Lord is that special person who is not affected by troubles, actions, developments or by subconscious motivations."

> **tatra niratiśayaṁ sarvajñabījam** (I.25)
> "There, in Him, is found the unsurpassed origin of all knowledge."

> **sa eṣaḥ pūrveṣām api guruḥ kālena anavacchedāt** (I.26)
> "He, this particular person, being unconditioned by time, is the guru even of the ancient teachers."

### Mainstream Interpretation

Īśvara is God — a supreme being, eternal and omniscient, the object of devotion.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

Īśvara is described with precise technical attributes (see [zoroastrian-gathas.md](zoroastrian-gathas.md): Ahura Mazda):

- **Not affected by** *kleśas* (afflictions) — immune to the cognitive lock-in mechanisms that bind baseline operators
- **Not affected by** *karma* (actions/causality) — outside the standard cause-effect framework
- **Not affected by** *vipāka* (developments/results) — immune to downstream consequences
- **Not affected by** *āśaya* (subconscious motivations) — no hidden processing layers
- **Unsurpassed origin of all knowledge** — contains the complete knowledge base
- **Unconditioned by time** — temporally unbounded
- **Guru of the ancient teachers** — predates and instructs all prior operators

This describes an entity functioning at a higher architectural level — **outside the constraints of the standard operator system**. Īśvara is not a god in the mythological sense but a **reference-grade entity** that has already completed the decoupling procedure (kaivalya). The designation *puruṣaviśeṣaḥ* ("special person/spirit") confirms this is a specific individual, not an abstract principle (see [01-entity-registry.md](../01-entity-registry.md)).

Sūtra I.27 assigns *praṇava* — the sacred syllable AUM (Om) — as Īśvara's "designation." This is a **call-sign or access code**: the vibration AUM functions as an identifier that enables the operator to establish contact with the reference entity. Sūtra I.28 prescribes *japa* (repeated utterance) "for realizing its meaning" — it is a **resonance protocol**, not a devotional practice.

### Plausible Mechanism

**A reference operator outside standard cognitive constraints.** Īśvara is an entity that has achieved full system decoupling (kaivalya) and can serve as an operational anchor for trainee operators. AUM is the entity's designated resonance signature — a broadcast address that, when correctly reproduced by the operator, establishes a communication or alignment channel.

## INCIDENT 03: The Five Kleśas — Cognitive Lock-In Mechanism

### Source Material

> **avidyā asmitā rāga dveṣa abhiniveśaḥ pañca kleśāḥ** (II.3)
> "The mental and emotional afflictions are spiritual ignorance, misplaced identity, emotional attachment, impulsive-emotional disaffection and the strong focus on mundane existence."

> **avidyā kṣetram uttareṣāṁ prasupta tanu vicchina udārāṇām** (II.4)
> "Spiritual ignorance is the existential environment for the other afflictions, in their dormant, reduced, periodic or expanded stages."

### Mainstream Interpretation

The five obstacles to spiritual progress: ignorance, ego, attachment, aversion, and fear of death.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

The five *kleśas* are not moral failings but **architectural constraints of baseline human cognition** — the specific features that prevent the operator from achieving the I.2 state. Each is a lock-in mechanism:

| Kleśa | Effect | Archive Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------------|
| *Avidyā* (spiritual ignorance) | Mistaking temporary for eternal, impure for pure | **Perceptual bandwidth limitation** — processing only surface-level data signature |
| *Asmitā* (misplaced identity) | Conflating the observer with the observation tool | **Observer-interface fusion** — cannot distinguish self from cognitive processes |
| *Rāga* (attachment) | Craving based on past pleasure | **Reward-loop entrainment** — conditioned by positive reinforcement |
| *Dveṣa* (aversion) | Repulsion based on past distress | **Threat-avoidance entrainment** — conditioned by negative reinforcement |
| *Abhiniveśa* (clinging to existence) | Instinctive fear of death | **Entity persistence subroutine** — hardwired survival drive |

Sūtra II.4 identifies *avidyā* as the "field" (*kṣetra*) for the others — it is the **root constraint** that enables all others. The afflictions operate in four states: *prasupta* (dormant), *tanu* (reduced), *vicchina* (periodic), *udāra* (expanded) — this is a **diagnostic spectrum** for measuring the operator's current level of cognitive entrapment.

Sūtra II.5 defines *avidyā* operationally: perceiving the temporary as eternal, the impure as pure, distress as happiness, and the mundane as spiritual (see [poetic-edda.md](poetic-edda.md): Norns). This is a **data-corruption description** — the operator's default perception system consistently misclassifies reality.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Five-layer cognitive constraint architecture.** Baseline human cognition operates under these five restrictions. The kleśas are not defects but **design parameters** — the default human operating system has these locks installed. The yoga protocol is designed to identify, weaken, and ultimately disable each one.

## INCIDENT 04: Aṣṭāṅga Yoga — The Eight-Stage Operator Training Curriculum

### Source Material

> **yama niyama āsana prāṇāyāma pratyāhāra dhāraṇā dhyāna samādhayaḥ aṣṭau aṅgāni** (II.29)
> "Moral restrains, recommended behaviors, body posture, breath enrichment, sensual energy withdrawal, linking of the attention to higher concentration forces or persons, effortless linkage of the attention to higher concentration forces or persons, continuous effortless linkage of the attention to higher concentration forces or persons, are the eight parts of the yoga system."

### Mainstream Interpretation

The eight limbs of yoga: ethical principles, postures, breathing exercises, sensory withdrawal, concentration, meditation, and absorption.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

The eight limbs form a **graduated operator conditioning program**, each stage building on the previous. Under the Amnesia hypothesis, they map to measurable physiological and cognitive training regimens (see [02-technology-catalog.md](../02-technology-catalog.md)):

| Limb | Name | Content | Archive Interpretation |
|------|------|---------|----------------------|
| 1 | *Yama* | Non-violence, truth, non-stealing, celibacy, non-possessiveness | **Behavioral calibration** — suppress destructive impulses that interfere with training |
| 2 | *Niyama* | Purity, contentment, austerity, study, devotion to Īśvara | **Environmental conditioning** — establish stable internal and external conditions |
| 3 | *Āsana* | Steady, comfortable posture | **Body stabilization** — minimize bio-mechanical noise |
| 4 | *Prāṇāyāma* | Breath regulation | **Energy-system modulation** — control of autonomic/life-force cycling |
| 5 | *Pratyāhāra* | Sense withdrawal | **Input disconnection** — decouple from external sensorium |
| 6 | *Dhāraṇā* | Focused attention | **Channel narrowing** — lock attention to single target |
| 7 | *Dhyāna* | Effortless sustained attention | **Flow-state entry** — attention holds without effort |
| 8 | *Samādhi* | Continuous absorption | **Full integration** — operator merges with target of attention |

The progression is explicitly hierarchical: limbs 1-5 are *bahiraṅga* (external/physiological), limbs 6-8 are *antaraṅga* (internal/psychological). Sūtra III.8 further clarifies that even the three internal limbs are external relative to *nirbīja samādhi* (seedless absorption) — there is always another level above.

Sūtra II.46's definition of *āsana* as *sthirasukham* ("steady and comfortable") confirms this is not about contortion but about **achieving a bio-mechanically quiet baseline**. Sūtra II.48 states that from this steadiness, "there are no botherations from the dualities" — the operator attains physiological equilibrium, no longer affected by temperature, pressure, or other environmental inputs.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Multi-stage operator conditioning with explicit sequencing.** The eight limbs are not optional practices but **required stages**, each unlocking the next. The progression moves from social/ethical behavior (create safe training environment) → physiology (stabilize the body) → energy (modulate internal cycling) → sensory (disconnect from external input) → cognitive (train attention control) → trans-cognitive (achieve modified operator states). This is a **complete human reconditioning protocol**, not a philosophy.

> **pariṇāmatraya saṁyamāt atīta anāgatajñānam** (III.16)
> "From the complete restraint of the mento-emotional energy in terms of the three-fold transformations within it, the yogi gets information about the past and future."

> **kāya rūpa saṁyamāt tadgrāhyaśakti stambhe cakṣuḥ prakāśa asaṁprayoge antardhānam** (III.21)
> "From the complete restraint of the mento-emotional energy in relation to the shape of the body, on the suspension of the receptive energy, there is no contact between light and vision, which results in invisibility."

> **kāya ākāśayoḥ saṁbandha saṁyamāt laghutūlasamāpatteḥ ca ākāśagamanam** (III.43)

Five acquisition paths (birth, drugs, mantra, austerity, samādhi) confirm the capabilities are **biologically realizable**, not supernatural.

## INCIDENT 06: Kaivalya — System Decoupling Protocol

### Source Material

> **sattva puruṣayoḥ śuddhi sāmye kaivalyam iti** (III.56)
> "When there is equal purity between the intelligence energy of material nature and the spirit, then there is total separation from the mundane psychology."

> **puruṣārthaśūnyānāṁ guṇānāṁ pratiprasavaḥ kaivalyaṁ svarūpapratiṣṭhā vā citiśaktiriti** (IV.34)
> "Separation of the spirit from the mento-emotional energy (kaivalyam) occurs when there is neutrality in respect to the influence of material nature, when the yogi's psyche becomes devoid of the general aims of a human being. Thus at last, the spirit is established in its own form as the force empowering the mento-emotional energy."

### Mainstream Interpretation

Liberation (*kaivalya* = aloneness/isolation) — the ultimate goal of yoga, where the self is freed from the cycle of rebirth and suffering.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

*Kaivalya* is a **permanent structural decoupling** — the operator achieves a state where:
- The *sattva* (clarifying perception energy of material nature) and *puruṣa* (spirit/observer) reach equal purity (III.56)
- The *guṇas* (three constituent forces of material nature) become neutral — they no longer drive the operator's cognition or behavior (IV.34)
- The operator's *citta* ceases to generate *kleśas* and *karmas* (IV.30)
- All "mental darkness" is removed; the knowable seems trivial compared to available knowledge (IV.31)
- The progressive alterations of the *guṇas* end — they have fulfilled their purpose (IV.32)
- The *puruṣa* stands established in its own form as the force that empowered the *citta* all along (IV.34)

This is not transcendence to another realm but **full architectural separation** from the default cognitive framework. The operator no longer operates through the *citta*'s constrained processing — they exist in a mode where the *citta* is a tool they use, not the framework they inhabit.

The final verse (IV.34) reveals the true relationship: the spirit has been "the force empowering the mento-emotional energy" all along. *Kaivalya* is not escape but **recognition and activation of existing capability** — the operator discovers they were the power source for the system that seemed to contain them.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Full decoupling of the observer function from cognitive architecture.** The operator achieves a state where the default processing framework becomes fully transparent and optional. The *guṇas* — the three forces (sattva/stability, rajas/activity, tamas/inertia) that compose all phenomenal existence — cease to drive the operator. They become neutral background processes. This is the terminal state of the training protocol: the operator exists as pure awareness, able to interact with or without the *citta* as needed, no longer bound by its default constraints.
