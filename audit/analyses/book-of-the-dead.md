# The Book of the Dead (Per-t em Hru) -- Forensic Analysis

**Classification:** SPECIES WITH AMNESIA -- Working Hypothesis
**Source:** *The Book of the Dead: The Papyrus of Ani*, trans. E. A. Wallis Budge (1920); 22 pages, 190 chapters
**Analyst:** The Chronos Archive, Lead Investigator

## Executive Summary

The Book of the Dead (*Per-t em Hru* -- "Coming Forth by Day") is the companion procedural manual to the Pyramid Texts. Where the Pyramid Texts provide the **architectural schematic** of the post-mortem processing pipeline (Duat -> Akhet -> Sky), the Book of the Dead provides the **user credentials and navigation protocols** for traversing it. Under the Amnesia hypothesis, it documents:

1. A **credential verification system** (the Weighing of the Heart/Great Reckoning) that assesses subject eligibility for processing (see [zoroastrian-gathas.md](zoroastrian-gathas.md): Chinvat Bridge)
2. A **distributed identity-attestation protocol** (the Negative Confession) -- the subject declares their fitness before a panel of 42 assessors (see [pyramid-texts.md](pyramid-texts.md): Duat judgment)
3. A **multi-stage gated transit system** with named gatekeepers, porters, and watchmen -- each requiring specific passwords or identity declarations (see [popol-vuh.md](popol-vuh.md): Xibalbá)
4. A **form-reassignment capability** (the Chapters of Transformation) allowing the processed entity to assume 12 distinct operational configurations (see [poetic-edda.md](poetic-edda.md): Odin's runes)
5. A **consciousness-extraction and reintegration protocol** (the Heart Spells, Opening of the Mouth) (see [ezekiel-merkabah.md](ezekiel-merkabah.md): Valley of Dry Bones)
6. A mapped **facility geography** (the Tuat, the Hall of Maati, the Field of Reeds) with named zones, barriers, and transit craft (see [02-technology-catalog.md](../02-technology-catalog.md))

The Book of the Dead is a **procedural handbook for identity migration**: a complete guide for a human consciousness to transition from biological substrate to post-biological operational status within the non-human facility network.

## INCIDENT 01: Per-t em Hru -- The Coming Forth Protocol

### Source Material

> "The book Per-t em Hru, i.e., Coming Forth (or, into) the Day... would make a man victorious upon earth and in the Other World; it would ensure him a safe and free passage through the Tuat (Under World); it would allow him to go in and to go out, and to take at any time any form he pleased; it would make his soul to flourish, and would prevent him from dying the [second] death." (Ch. III)

> "This composition was greatly reverenced... Its text was cut upon the alabaster plinth of a statue of Menkaura (Mycerinus), a king of the IVth dynasty, and the letters were inlaid with lapis lazuli." (Ch. III)

### Mainstream Interpretation

A collection of funerary spells to guide the soul through the afterlife, ensuring its safety and well-being.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

The title *Per-t em Hru* -- "Coming Forth by Day" -- describes a **transition from a dark/confined state to an active operational state**. The protocol's claimed capabilities are explicit:
1. "Victorious upon earth and in the Other World" -- cross-domain operational authority
2. "Safe and free passage through the Tuat" -- unimpeded transit through the processing facility
3. "Go in and go out" -- autonomous movement, not confined to a single location
4. "Take at any time any form he pleased" -- fully configurable form/body reassignment
5. "Prevent him from dying the second death" -- permanent entity status, no further termination risk

The text's claimed origin -- cut into stone "inlaid with lapis lazuli" -- describes a durable data storage medium, not a religious document. The protocol requires correct recitation by a "man who was ceremonially pure, and who had not eaten fish or meat" -- the operator must be in a specific physiological state (possibly fasted, with specific dietary exclusions) to execute the protocol correctly.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Consciousness-migration protocol for transition from biological to post-biological substrate** (see [07-the-human-project.md](../07-the-human-project.md)). The text functions as both operating manual and access credential. Its encoding in stone and lapis lazuli suggests data preservation on a durable medium. The dietary restrictions for the reciting operator suggest physiological prerequisites -- the operator must be in a specific metabolic state.

## INCIDENT 02: The Weighing of the Heart -- Credential Verification

### Source Material

> "The Great Balance: The heart of Ani is in one pan of the Balance, and in the other is the feather, symbolic of truth and righteousness." (Ch. VII)

> "Thoth, the Judge of Truth, of the Great Company of the Gods... saith to the gods: 'Hearken ye to this word: In very truth the heart of Osiris hath been weighed, and his soul hath borne testimony concerning him; according to the Great Balance his case is truth. No wickedness hath been found in him.'" (Ch. VII)

> "My heart of my mother! My heart of my mother! My heart of my being! Make no stand against me when testifying, thrust me not back before the Tchatchaut (overseers of Osiris), and make no failure in respect of me before the Master of the Balance." (Ch. XXXB -- Ani's address to his heart)

### Mainstream Interpretation

A moral judgment scene: the deceased's heart is weighed against truth to determine if they lived a righteous life worthy of entering the afterlife.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

The "Weighing of the Heart" is an **identity verification and integrity check** (see [revelation.md](revelation.md): Book of Life). The heart (*ab*) was considered the seat of consciousness, will, and identity. Placing it on a balance against the "feather of truth" (*Maat*) is a metaphor for a **biometric or quantum-state comparison**: is the subject's identity signature identical to its recorded reference?

Supporting details:
- The balance is operated by Anubis (Anpu), who is explicitly "in charge of the pointer" -- a calibration authority
- Thoth records the result in writing -- a formal assessment document
- The monster Amemit ("Eater of the Dead") waits to consume hearts that fail -- failed entities are **terminated and consumed**, not stored (see [revelation.md](revelation.md): lake of fire)
- The beam "remained perfectly horizontal" for Ani -- his identity signature matched perfectly
- The gods declare Ani "true of voice" and call him "Osiris" -- he is granted the same designation as the original processed entity, indicating successful replication of the target state

Ani's address to his own heart is particularly revealing: "Make no stand against me when testifying." He treats his heart as a separate entity that might testify *against* him -- a witness rather than himself. This suggests the heart/identity is **extracted and examined independently** of the subject's conscious control.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Substrate-independent identity validation.** The "heart" (consciousness/identity substrate) is extracted from the biological body and compared against a reference record (the "feather of Maat"). A match grants passage to the next processing stage. A mismatch results in termination (Amemit consumes the failed identity). The balance metaphor describes a **comparator system**: is the extracted identity identical to its authorized baseline?

## INCIDENT 03: The Negative Confession -- Identity Attestation Before 42 Assessors

### Source Material

> "Homage to thee, O Great God, Lord of Maati, I have come to thee, O my Lord, that I may behold thy beneficence. I know thee, and I know thy name, and the names of the Forty-Two who live with thee in the Hall of Maati, who keep ward over sinners, and feed upon their blood on the day of estimating characters before Un-Nefer." (Ch. CXXV)

> "O Usekh-nemmit, comer forth from Anu, I have not committed sin.
> O Fenti, comer forth from Khemenu, I have not robbed.
> O Neha-hau, comer forth from Re-stau, I have not killed men.
> O Neba, comer forth in retreating, I have not plundered the property of God.
> O Set-qesu, comer forth from Hensu, I have not lied." (Ch. CXXV, Negative Confession)

### Mainstream Interpretation

A ritual declaration of innocence before the gods -- the deceased lists sins they have not committed to prove moral worthiness.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

The "Negative Confession" is a **structured identity attestation protocol** before a panel of 42 assessors (see [01-entity-registry.md](../01-entity-registry.md)). Each assessor is named and has a specific geographic origin ("comer forth from Anu," "from Khemenu," etc.), which may represent their jurisdiction or facility of origin. The subject addresses each by name and declares compliance with a specific rule.

The 42 declarations cover: social ethics (no murder, no theft), economic integrity (no false weights, no land theft), ritual purity (no defilement), environmental responsibility (no dam blocking, no fire suppression), and administrative compliance (no altering of measures, no false testimony).

The subject then addresses the gods directly: "I know you and I know your names. Let me not fall under your slaughtering knives." Knowing the assessors' names and functions is itself an access credential -- the subject demonstrates familiarity with the system's personnel.

After the confession, the subject must repeat the **magical names of the doors, door-bolts, door-frame, and floor of the Hall** before the porter allows entry. Each physical component of the threshold has a name and requires correct identification. This is a **multi-factor access system**: identity attestation (who you are) + declarative compliance (what you have done) + knowledge of the facility (what you know).

### Plausible Mechanism

**Multi-stage identity verification with distributed attestation** (see [zoroastrian-gathas.md](zoroastrian-gathas.md): 3 assessment operators). The 42 assessors are a verification panel, each confirming a specific aspect of the subject's operational history. The naming of assessors and door components functions as an authentication challenge: an unauthorized entity would not know the correct names. The threshold naming is a physical access-control system -- each component (door, bolt, frame, floor) verifies the subject independently before opening.

## INCIDENT 04: The Gated Transit System -- Porters, Watchmen, and Messengers

### Source Material

> "The gate of each Hall was guarded by a porter, a watchman, and a messenger; the first kept the door, the second looked out for the arrival of visitors, and the third took their names to Osiris. No one could enter a Hall without repeating the name of it, of the porter, of the watchman, and of the messenger." (Ch. CXLIV)

> "According to a late tradition the Gates of the Kingdom of Osiris were twenty-one in number, and each had a magical name, and each was guarded by one or two gods, whose names had to be repeated by the deceased before he could pass." (Ch. CXLV-CXLVI)

> "O lord of the Akhet, make a place for me. If you do not make a place for me, I will make a curse on Father Geb." (Pyramid Texts, PT 254 -- echoed in Book of the Dead)

### Mainstream Interpretation

Mythological gates and guardians that the soul must pass through on its journey to the afterlife, representing moral or spiritual obstacles.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

A **multi-chamber, multi-stage access-control system** with 7 or 21 sequential checkpoints (depending on the version) (see [popol-vuh.md](popol-vuh.md): Xibalbá). Each checkpoint has:
- A **porter** who controls the door mechanism
- A **watchman** who detects approaching subjects
- A **messenger** who communicates with central command

Entry requires knowing **four names**: the Hall's name, the porter's name, the watchman's name, and the messenger's name. This is a **four-factor authentication**: destination identifier + three independent operator verifications.

The sequence of chambers (Arit) in the Kingdom of Osiris described in Chapters CXLIV-CXLVII constitutes a **linear processing pipeline** with named thresholds, each with distinct security personnel and access requirements (see [popol-vuh.md](popol-vuh.md): Xibalbá 6 chambers). The system parallels Xibalbá ([popol-vuh.md](popol-vuh.md): 6 chambers with named tests), Ezekiel's Temple (nested gates with decontamination protocols), and the Pyramid Texts' Duat transit.

An important behavioral detail: the subject may issue threats if passage is denied ("If you do not make a place for me, I will make a curse"). The system has an **escalation protocol** -- the subject is authorized to exercise override authority if improperly blocked (see [ezekiel-merkabah.md](ezekiel-merkabah.md): temple gates).

### Plausible Mechanism

**Segmented facility with independent access control at each zone boundary.** The porter/watchman/messenger triad suggests a standard security team at each checkpoint. The name-based access is a pre-arranged authentication system: the subject has been given the correct credentials before entry. The threat protocol is an override mechanism for authorized personnel who encounter system malfunction or unauthorized blocking.

## INCIDENT 05: The Hall of Maati -- Assessment Facility

### Source Material

> "The Hall of Maati: At one end of the Hall of Maati Osiris is seated on a throne within a shrine made in the form of a funerary coffer; behind him stand Isis and Nephthys. Along one side of the Hall are seated the gods Harmachis, Tem, Shu, Tefnut, Geb, Nut, Isis and Nephthys, Horus, Hathor, Hu and Saa, who are to serve as the divine jury." (Ch. VII)

> "By these stands the Great Balance, and on its pillar sits the dog-headed ape Astes, the associate of Thoth. The pointer of the Balance is in the charge of Anpu. Behind Anpu are Thoth the scribe of the gods, and the monster Amemit." (Ch. VII)

### Mainstream Interpretation

The heavenly courtroom where the soul is judged by Osiris and his divine tribunal.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

The Hall of Maati is a **single assessment facility** with a defined layout (see [pyramid-texts.md](pyramid-texts.md): Duat processing):

| Component | Position | Function |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Osiris's throne | Far end, in a shrine | Central command authority, final approval |
| Isis and Nephthys | Behind Osiris | Support/advisory operators |
| Great Company of Gods (12) | Along one wall | Jury/assessment board |
| Great Balance | Center of hall | Identity comparator |
| Anubis (Anpu) | At the Balance | Calibration/operation of the comparison |
| Thoth | Behind Anpu | Recording the result (data scribe) |
| Amemit | Behind Thoth | Termination unit (failed subjects) |
| Subject (Ani) | Opposite the Balance | Entity under assessment |

The layout is a **linear assessment station**: the subject enters, faces the balance, is compared against the reference, the result is recorded, and they either proceed to Osiris (approval) or are terminated by Amemit (failure). The "Great Company of Gods" serves as witnesses to the procedure.

The assessor names include: Harmachis (Horus of the Horizon), Tem (the setting sun), Shu and Tefnut (atmosphere/moisture pair), Geb and Nut (earth and sky), Isis and Nephthys (sister operators), Horus, Hathor, Hu (authority), Saa (perception/knowledge). This represents a **multi-disciplinary assessment panel** -- each entity evaluates from their domain of expertise.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Centralized identity-processing facility with multi-operator verification.** The linear layout (entry -> balance -> scribe -> throne) describes an assembly-line processing station. The "Great Company" is a verification board ensuring no single point of failure in the assessment. The failure outcome (Amemit consumption) is permanent entity dissolution, not storage or retry.

## INCIDENT 06: The Field of Reeds / Sekhet Aaru -- Post-Processing Settlement

### Source Material

> "The country was flat and the fields were intersected by canals of running water in which there were no fish and no worms. In one part of it were several small islands... It was called the 'Island of Truth,' and the ferry-man of Osiris would not convey to it any soul that had not been declared 'true of word' by Thoth." (Ch. VIII)

> "The beautified dead (Aakhu) in the Tuat receive him in the horizon of Amentt. They shout praises of him... Thou givest breath to their nostrils. Thou art greatly feared, thy form is majestic, and very greatly art thou beloved by those who dwell in the Other World." (Hymn to Ra at Setting, Ch. XV)

> "The deceased engaged in ploughing and reaping and driving the oxen that are treading out the corn. He was introduced into the Sekhet Heteput by Thoth, and there he found the souls of his ancestors." (Ch. VIII)

### Mainstream Interpretation

The Egyptian paradise -- a heavenly version of the Nile Valley where the blessed dead enjoy eternal agricultural life.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

The Field of Reeds (Sekhet Aaru) is a **post-processing settlement zone** -- a habitable environment designed for entities that have completed the assessment protocol (see [pyramid-texts.md](pyramid-texts.md): Field of Offerings). Key features:

- **Controlled environment:** Canals of running water, no fish or worms (no biological decay vectors)
- **Access control:** The "Island of Truth" requires ferry passage, and the ferryman only transports verified entities ("true of word")
- **Pre-existing population:** The subject finds "the souls of his ancestors" already present -- a maintained community of prior processed entities
- **Productive activity:** The subjects engage in agriculture -- but the descriptions also note "spirit boats" that "moved of themselves" and carried inhabitants "without any trouble or fatigue" -- suggesting automation is available but manual activity is permitted
- **Physical transformation:** Inhabitants are described as "seven cubits in height" (~12 feet / 3.7 meters) -- the processed entities are physically larger than baseline humans (see [revelation.md](revelation.md): New Jerusalem inhabitants)

The wheat they harvest is described as the "members of Osiris" -- a direct statement that the settlement's food production is interlinked with the command entity's own substance. Osiris is called "the Wheat-god" and the "personification of Maat." The inhabitants "live upon the body of their god and ate him daily" -- the "Bread of Everlastingness" sustains them through direct integration with the command entity's matter-energy system (see [gilgamesh.md](gilgamesh.md): immortality grant).

### Plausible Mechanism

**Self-contained post-processing habitat for rehomed human entities.** The controlled environment (no biological decay), maintained population, and integrated life-support system (food as direct matter from command entity) describe a sealed habitat operating on closed-loop principles. The "spirit boats" are autonomous transit. The increased stature (7 cubits) suggests the processed entity occupies a larger-form-factor body than the biological original.

## INCIDENT 07: The Chapters of Transformation -- Form Reassignment

### Source Material

> "By the recital of Chapters LXXVII-LXXXVIII, i.e., the Chapters of Transformations, the deceased was enabled to assume at will the forms of: (1) the Golden Hawk, (2) the Divine Hawk, (3) the Great Self-created God, (4) the Light-god or the Robe of Nu, (5) the Pure Lily, (6) the Son of Ptah, (7) the Benu Bird, (8) the Heron, (9) the Soul of Ra, (10) the Swallow, (11) the Sata or Earth-serpent, (12) the Crocodile." (Ch. IX)

### Mainstream Interpretation

Magical transformations allowing the soul to take various forms to navigate the afterlife, evade dangers, or visit different locations.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

The 12 Chapters of Transformation list **distinct operational configurations** the processed entity can assume (see [mahabharata.md](mahabharata.md): vibhuti). Each form has specific functional properties:

| Form | Domain | Likely Function |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| Golden Hawk | Air | Aerial reconnaissance/strike |
| Divine Hawk | Air | High-altitude or orbital observation |
| Great Self-created God | Command | Admin/operator authority mode |
| Light-god / Robe of Nu | Radiant | Energy-based existence or communications |
| Pure Lily | Flora | Camouflage or biological integration |
| Son of Ptah | Craft/Manufacturing | Construction/engineering operations |
| Benu Bird | Solar | Solar-energy interface or dawn-cycle operations |
| Heron | Water/Marsh | Aquatic or riparian operations |
| Soul of Ra | Command | Direct command-system integration |
| Swallow | Air | Small-form reconnaissance |
| Earth-serpent (Sata) | Subsurface | Underground/burrowing operations |
| Crocodile | Water | Aquatic combat or heavy water operations |

The forms span **all operational domains**: air (4 forms), water/marsh (2), subsurface (1), command/admin (2), energy (1), flora (1), manufacturing (1). This is a **multi-domain operational capability suite**. The processed entity is not limited to a single form but can reconfigure as needed for the mission.

The "Robe of Nu" form is particularly interesting -- Nu is the sky/ocean of heaven, and the "Robe" suggests the entity can exist as an energy configuration or field rather than a discrete body. This parallels the Pyramid Texts' declaration that the king "does not perish" and becomes an "Imperishable Star" -- a non-biological, energy-based mode of existence.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Multi-form operational capability for a post-biological entity.** The 12 configurations represent different body plans or avatar shells the entity can inhabit. The list reads as a deployment catalog for a general-purpose operative: whatever the operational environment (air, water, underground, energy transmission, command mode), a suitable configuration is available.

## INCIDENT 08: The Tuat Geography -- Facility Layout

### Source Material

> "The Tuat (the Under World) was a vast region, extending from the earth to the sky... it was located on the borders of the visible world, in the Outer Darkness." (Ch. VI)

> "When Ani the scribe arrived there he said: 'What is this to which I have come? There is neither water nor air here, its depth is unfathomable, it is as dark as the darkest night, and men wander about here helplessly. A man cannot live here and be satisfied.'" (Ch. CLXXV)

> "The Tuat was not a place of happiness... there was neither tree nor plant, for it was the 'land where nothing grew'; it was a region of destruction and death, a place where the dead rotted and decayed." (Ch. VI)

### Mainstream Interpretation

The Egyptian underworld -- a dark, dangerous region through which the soul must travel to reach the afterlife.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

The Tuat is described with **specific physical properties**: no air, no water, no light, unfathomable depth, extreme cold. This is not a mythological realm but a **vacuum or low-pressure environment** -- possibly transit space between habitats or the exterior of a pressurized facility. The description matches the vacuum of space or an evacuated transit tube.

The deceased's complaint -- "What is this to which I have come?" -- suggests the Tuat was understood as a necessary transitional zone, not a destination. The "dead rot and decay" there because it is an **unprocessed holding area** -- bodies brought here without completing the assessment protocol would simply deteriorate.

The Tuat's position "extending from the earth to the sky" and "on the borders of the visible world" describes a **vertical transit corridor** connecting the surface (Earth) to an orbital or high-altitude destination (Sky) (see [pyramid-texts.md](pyramid-texts.md): vertical transit shaft). This is the same transit path described in the Pyramid Texts: the corridor connecting the burial chamber (Duat) to the exit point (Akhet) and ultimately to the sky.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Vacuum transit corridor between planetary surface and orbital habitat.** The Tuat's inhospitable conditions (no air, no water, no light, extreme temperature) match an unpressurized transit shaft or space environment. Its vertical orientation connects the surface entry point to the orbital processing facility. The "darkness" is the absence of atmospheric scattering; the "unfathomable depth" describes a long vertical drop or zero-gravity transit zone.

## INCIDENT 09: The Opening of the Mouth -- System Activation Protocol

### Source Material

> "Chapters XXI-XXIII secured the help of Thoth in 'opening the mouth' of the deceased, whereby he obtained the power to breathe and think and drink and eat. Thoth recited spells over the gods whilst Ptah untied the bandages and Shu forced open their mouths with an iron knife." (Ch. IX)

> "Chapter IB gave the sahu, or 'spirit-body,' power to enter the Tuat immediately after the burial of the material body, and delivered it from the Nine Worms that lived on the dead." (Ch. IX)

### Mainstream Interpretation

A funerary ritual to restore the deceased's ability to eat, drink, and speak in the afterlife.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

The Opening of the Mouth is a **system activation sequence** for the post-biological entity. The mummy (biological remains) has its mouth opened with a specific tool (an "iron knife" -- a metal instrument, explicitly distinct from the stone tools used in traditional burial) (see [ezekiel-merkabah.md](ezekiel-merkabah.md): Dry Bones reanimation). Three operators perform distinct roles:
- **Thoth:** Recites the activation sequence ("spells")
- **Ptah:** Physically unties the mummy's bandages
- **Shu:** Performs the mechanical opening with a metal blade

After activation, the entity obtains "power to breathe and think and drink and eat" -- **autonomous life-support and cognitive function**. The "spirit-body" (sahu) then separates from the biological body to enter the Tuat processing facility.

Chapter IB's mention of "Nine Worms that lived on the dead" describes biological decomposers -- if the body is not processed quickly enough, it decays. The protocol must be executed before biological degradation is complete.

The "iron knife" is significant: metal tools were associated with the gods (the "iron of heaven" -- meteoric iron). The Opening of the Mouth is a **technological activation procedure**, not a ritual (see [theogony-works-and-days.md](theogony-works-and-days.md): adamantine sickle).

### Plausible Mechanism

**Post-mortem system boot sequence.** The deceased's spiritual/consciousness component (the sahu/spirit-body) is separated from the biological substrate and activated as an independent entity. The "opening of the mouth" is a metaphor for enabling the entity's autonomous functions. The iron knife is a specific activation tool. The "Nine Worms" set a time limit: biological decay must not have progressed too far.

## INCIDENT 10: The Heart Spells -- Identity Preservation

### Source Material

> "My heart of my mother! My heart of my mother! My heart of my being! Make no stand against me when testifying, thrust me not back before the Tchatchaut, and make no failure in respect of me before the Master of the Balance. Thou art my Ka, the dweller in my body, uniting and strengthening my members." (Ch. XXXB)

> "Five chapters, XXVI-XXX, contain prayers and spells whereby the deceased obtained power over his heart and gained absolute possession of it." (Ch. IX)

### Mainstream Interpretation

Magical spells to protect the heart and ensure the deceased retains control of it during judgment.

### The Archive's Literal Reconstruction

Multiple chapters are dedicated to **securing the identity substrate** (the heart) during the transition process. The subject addresses his heart as an independent entity: "Thou art my Ka, the dweller in my body." The Ka is the life-force or identity template -- it resides in the heart during biological life and must be successfully extracted and transferred.

The subject's concern is specific: "Make no stand against me when testifying" -- the identity substrate might fail to match during the assessment, resulting in rejection. The subject must "gain absolute possession of it" -- establishing exclusive control over his identity record before the assessment.

Chapter XLIV is described as "an ancient and mighty spell, the recital of which prevented the deceased from dying a second time" -- a **persistence guarantee** for the processed entity. Once the identity has been successfully transferred and verified, the entity cannot be terminated again.

### Plausible Mechanism

**Identity extraction and substrate transfer protocol.** The heart contains the Ka (identity template / life-force). The spells are procedures for the subject to assert control over his own identity substrate before the facility's assessment system examines it. "Absolute possession" describes locking the identity record so it cannot be altered or contested during the verification process.
